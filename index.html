What New York State Businesses Have Been Most Affected During COVID-19?

NYC Department of Labor posts New York State WARN notices, among which hospitality and retail industries seem to be the hardest hit during COVID-19.

Restaurants and hotels among those businesses most impacted during pandemic.

By Shehzil Zahid Shehzil Zahid
Published May 18, 2020 8:00 pm

The New York State Department of Labor

The New York State Worker Adjustment and Retraining (WARN) Act requires covered businesses to provide early warnings of closures and layoffs to employees, representatives, the Department of Labor, and others. The goal is to give workers and their families time to transition and find new jobs or alternatives.

According to the NYC WARN notices, the industry/business that has been most affected in New York State is the hospitality industry, which includes restaurants, resorts and bars. 708 hospitality businesses have shut down either permenantly or temporarily since COVID-19.

Broadly speaking, the hospitality industry includes lodging, food and drink service, event planning, theme parks, and transportation.

This bar chart shows the top five industries that have suffered the most since the state-mandated order to close businesses during the pandemic. The bar chart shows a disproportionate number of hospitality businesses that have shut down either temporarily or permanently since the closure, compared to other businesses like retail, health care, auto dealerships and manufacturing.

Compared to hospitality businesses, only 98 retail businesses have shut down temporarily or permanently. This includes clothing stores, shoe stores and department stores, among others.

Similarly, 72 health care businesses have been affected with temporary or permanent closures. These businesses include hospitals, eye doctors, gyms and dentists, among others.

Auto dealerships and manufacturing round out the top 5 most affected New York State businesses, with 42 and 36 temporary or permanent closures since the pandemic began.
